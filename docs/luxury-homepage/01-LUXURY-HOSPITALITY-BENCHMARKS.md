# 01 - Luxury Hospitality and Private Club Benchmarks

Method (honest): this environment cannot capture live screenshots of third-party sites, and browsing every property in one pass is impractical. This is a representative synthesis from direct reference and current knowledge of these brands' digital languages. Screenshots were not captured; unverifiable specifics are framed as principles, not asserted about a page.

Territories: Aman, Rosewood, Belmond, Design Hotels, The Standard, The Ned, Soho House, Auberge, Capella, Edition, and independent boutique hotels and destination restaurants.

- **First screen:** one large quiet image, a small wordmark, one line of intent, minimal chrome. -> A near-full-height macro Pour, restrained wordmark, one headline. No search bar, no chips.
- **Service language:** warm second person; exclusivity by tone, not gatekeeping. -> "Ask the concierge," "Tonight's selection," "Keep it," "Send the evening." Public access, private feeling.
- **Typography:** a refined serif for a few moments; a clean sans elsewhere; deliberate tracking. -> Fraunces + Inter; champagne small-caps labels.
- **Pacing:** long rhythm alternating full-bleed image and calm reading panels. -> Obsidian sections alternate with porcelain.
- **Navigation:** a restrained expanding index with a featured image and grouped links; never colored icon grids. -> The concierge-index mega-nav.
- **Motion / decisions:** soft masks and fades (350 to 650 ms); calm guided steps. -> The concierge and House Choice reveal a note like a room-service card.
- **Mobile:** one strong image, real whitespace, fast legible menu; no shrunken grid.
- **Performance:** heavy video and huge heroes hurt on cellular; we use CSS/SVG art instead.
- **Do not imitate:** literal members-only gatekeeping, mystery nav, or 100vh of empty hero.
