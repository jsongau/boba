# 06 - Luxury Mobile Principles (applied)

Designed at 390px first, then scaled up. Not a shrunken desktop.

- **One strong image, never blank.** The hero Pour is CSS/SVG, so it paints instantly with no load gap; the arrival note floats over it.
- **First recommendation early.** Tonight's Selection is in the first mobile screen, under the headline.
- **No chip walls, no miniature cards.** Content is menu-sheet rows and full-width panels.
- **44px targets.** All buttons meet 44px min height; the drawer uses large summaries.
- **No hover dependency.** Mega-nav becomes a details-based drawer with search and recent selections; tap-first.
- **Save/share in reach.** "Keep it" and "Send" on every selection and in the Black Book.
- **Map never blocks the journey.** No embedded map on the homepage; directions open the native app on demand.
- **Native controls.** City and preference inputs are native select and segmented buttons.
- **Reduced motion respected.** Reveal and step animations disabled; content stays visible.

Breakpoints exercised: 320 / 375 / 390 / 430 (single column, stacked hero, floating note); 768 / 860 (two-column city splits, sheet columns); 1000 (two-column arrival); 1040 (desktop mega-nav); 1240 (max width). Per-breakpoint reasoning in doc 10.
