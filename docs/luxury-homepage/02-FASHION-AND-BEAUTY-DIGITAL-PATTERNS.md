# 02 - Fashion, Beauty, Jewelry and Fragrance Patterns

Method: representative synthesis from established knowledge of these brands' web languages; no third-party screenshots captured here. Principles, not layouts.

Territories: Aesop, Le Labo, Byredo, Diptyque, Loewe, Bottega Veneta, Jacquemus, Cartier, Hermes, Acne Studios, independent studios.

1. **Controlled palette.** One or two colors carry a whole site. -> Obsidian + porcelain + one editorial color per moment.
2. **Object photography and macro material.** Close texture creates desire. -> The macro Pour and ingredient-texture fields until licensed photography exists (doc 09).
3. **Asymmetry with strict alignment.** Off-center on a precise grid. -> The arrival hero and city splits.
4. **Type hierarchy over decoration.** Big serif line, small tracked labels, space. -> Fraunces + tracked labels.
5. **Little copy, high evocation.** A few words per screen. -> "Silky oolong, toasted sugar, and a quieter room."
6. **Saving feels like keeping an object.** -> The Black Book.
7. **Square-ish shapes, hairlines.** Not 20px rounded cards. -> 3 to 6px radius, hairline dividers, menu-sheet rows.

Do not turn a usable directory into an unusable fashion portfolio; the directory stays practical on its own route.
