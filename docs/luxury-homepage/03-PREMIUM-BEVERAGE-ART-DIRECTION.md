# 03 - Premium Beverage Art Direction

Method: synthesis from established knowledge of premium tea/coffee/Champagne brands (Mariage Freres, Fortnum & Mason, Ippodo, Kettl, Postcard Teas, Ruinart, Dom Perignon, The Macallan, Seedlip, specialty roasters). No third-party screenshots captured here.

## How liquid is made to feel luxurious
- Deep blacks and a single warm light; tea color glows, condensation and glass catch a highlight.
- Macro and ritual: the pour, the steam, the settling of leaves or pearls; process over product-on-white.
- Provenance and tasting notes, honestly stated; nothing invented.
- Small, dated seasonal sets, not an endless catalog.

## Making boba feel luxurious without misrepresenting it
- Render the drink as a considered object: smoked-glass vessel, deep tea gradient, cream ribbon, settling pearls, a rim of light. This is the site's signature Pour.
- Keep the joy; the register is a good cocktail bar, not a solemn ceremony.
- Never dress a cheap claim in luxury type. Caffeine/dairy only where stated; no prices unless verified; "seasonal" only for official seasonal items.
- Every art-directed field is labelled "editorial rendering," so a rendering is never mistaken for a photo of a specific real menu item.
