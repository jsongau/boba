# Boba Night — Engagement Language Guide

How to write every user-facing line on bobanight.com: headings, CTAs, microcopy, floaters, empty states, and section ledes. Researched July 2026 from hospitality conversion copy (Airbnb, hotel and restaurant brand systems), CTA testing literature (WordStream, ContentVerve first-person tests, Matchbox microcopy studies), and group-ordering copy analysis. Sources listed at the end. This guide layers on top of the CoverCapy anti-AI writing rules, which still apply everywhere.

## The voice in one line

A well-connected friend who runs the best room in town: generous, specific, never pushy, and glad you came.

## The ten rules

**1. Verbs of receiving, never verbs of labor.**
CTAs use get, see, find, open, show. Never submit, click, view, go, learn more. The click should read as receiving something, not operating software.
Weak: "View results." Strong: "See who's open past midnight."

**2. Verb plus the exact thing gained.**
Every CTA names its payoff. "See tonight's picks," "Open the boba map," "Find boba near me." If the button could sit on any website, it is too generic for this one.

**3. First person converts.**
Where a CTA is personal, write it as the user's own decision: "Show me what's open." ContentVerve's published test on first-person button copy measured a 90 percent lift over second-person. Use sparingly, on the highest-intent buttons only.

**4. Name the outcome in the user's night, not the feature.**
Weak: "Advanced filtering." Strong: "Closest first, open now on top."

**5. Reassurance under the ask.**
Every high-intent CTA earns a small line beneath it that kills the hidden objection: "Hours checked this week." "No account needed." "Used once, never saved." This is already house style in the finder; extend it everywhere.

**6. Real urgency only, and only from facts.**
Closing times are legitimate urgency for a night guide: "Closes at 1am, 40 minutes left to order." Manufactured pressure is banned: no "don't miss out," no "hurry," no countdown theatrics.

**7. Celebrate rarity, never threaten with it.**
Airbnb frames scarcity as a find ("a rare find"); booking-engine copy frames it as a threat ("selling fast"). We celebrate: "Late-night win: three rooms near Cypress are still pouring."

**8. The occasion never needs a permission slip.**
Never "any excuse to," "just because," "might as well," "why not." These frame time with people as something requiring justification. State the occasion as fact and let it be enough.
Weak: "any excuse to gather." Strong: "Tuesday, 11pm, no occasion. That's the occasion."

**9. Name the people and the ritual, not the abstraction.**
"The whole booth," "the group chat," "the debrief after KBBQ," "the friend who swore they weren't thirsty." Never "quality time," "good vibes," "sharing is caring." One concrete image outworks any amount of stock warmth.

**10. Generosity math, not per-head math.**
Group copy promises abundance, not servings. Weak: "serves 6." Strong: "Enough for the crew, plus the friend who swore they weren't thirsty." Be the host holding the door open, never the brand pretending it is in the group chat.

## Banned list

Minimizers: just, even just, only (as apology), any excuse to, just because, no big deal, a quick boba run, might as well, if you're bored, why not, really, actually and basically as emphasis.

Pressure: don't miss out, hurry, last chance, you have to, drag your friends, tag a friend who, spots filling up.

Stock warmth: good vibes, made with love, sharing is caring, treat yourself (unless earned by a specific image).

Plus everything on the CoverCapy anti-AI banned list (elevate, delve, seamless, vibrant, and the rest), em-dash separators, arrows, and underlined text links.

## Approved lines, ready to use

Bulk floater: "Boba for the whole crew" over "One jug pours six. Enough for the crew, plus the friend who swore they weren't thirsty."

Finder reassurance: "Used once, in your browser, to sort by distance. Nothing saved or shared."

Open-late framing: "Open past midnight. See who's still pouring."

Empty state, everything closed: "Everything just closed. The late-night guide has you covered."

Group scene-setter: "You're all in the booth, lights low, and the next round is already being negotiated."

Occasion line: "Tuesday, 11pm, no occasion. That's the occasion."

Search placeholder pattern: prompt the real query, "Try taro slush near Cypress," not "Search…"

## The test before shipping any line

Read it aloud and ask: does this sound like a generous host, or like software, or like an ad? If a line apologizes for the plan, counts servings, or pressures anyone, rewrite it. If it could appear on any other website unchanged, it is not done.
