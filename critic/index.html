The Pearl Ratings

Southern California has review counts. It does not have a critic. The Pearl Ratings are Boba Night's editorial verdicts on boba and dessert houses — visited in person, paid for in full, and rated the way the old guides rated kitchens: on repeat visits, without warning, and without mercy for a watery jelly.

N
Nathan J. SongHouse Critic · dessert desk lead · grass jelly loyalist

The scale

●
One PearlWorth a detour on the way home. The drink or the bowl does one thing memorably right.
●●
Two PearlsWorth crossing a county line. The craft holds up on a second and third visit, at full price, on a bad day.
●●●
Three PearlsWorth planning the weekend around. The rare rooms where the whole menu means it.
The Jelly Mark 🌿 — a separate distinction, awarded stingily, for grass jelly done properly: brewed herbal depth, a clean bitter edge, a set that quivers but never slumps. The desk considers this its founding beat.

How the desk works

Visits are unannounced and paid out of pocket — shops can't buy pearls, comp them, or know the critic is in the room. No rating publishes before repeat visits. Verdicts are labeled opinions, and anything stated as fact (an address, an hour, a menu item) is verified against a primary source first, same as everywhere on Boba Night. When the desk gets one wrong, the review says so and stays up.
